# 📘 MedVocab-Pro: Clinical Pharmacology Glossary

## 🧬 Term: First-pass effect

---

### 🔍 1. Etymology & Lexicology
* **Root:** Derived from English *First* + *Pass* (Latin *passus*) + *Effect* (Latin *effectus*).
* **Context:** Describes the phenomenon of drug metabolism occurring before the agent reaches systemic circulation.

---

### 🏥 2. Clinical Definition
**First-pass effect** (or *Presystemic Metabolism*) is a process where the concentration of a drug is greatly reduced before it reaches the systemic circulation. 
* **Primary Site:** Predominantly occurs in the **liver** (hepatic metabolism).
* **Mechanism:** Oral drugs are absorbed from the GI tract into the **portal vein**, passing through the liver where enzymes (CYP450) metabolize them.



---

### 💊 3. Pharmacological Context
* **Bioavailability ($F$):** High first-pass effect leads to low systemic bioavailability.
* **Clinical Strategy:** Drugs with extensive first-pass metabolism (e.g., *Nitroglycerin*, *Isosorbide dinitrate*) are administered via **sublingual**, **transdermal**, or **IV** routes to bypass the liver.



---

### 🎓 4. Academic Usage (C2 Level)
> *"The extensive hepatic **first-pass effect** of propranolol necessitates significantly higher oral doses compared to intravenous administration to achieve therapeutic plasma concentrations."*
